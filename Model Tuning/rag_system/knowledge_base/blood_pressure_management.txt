Blood Pressure Management in Diabetes

Blood Pressure Categories:
- Normal: Less than 120/80 mmHg
- Elevated: 120-129/<80 mmHg
- Stage 1 Hypertension: 130-139/80-89 mmHg
- Stage 2 Hypertension: ≥140/≥90 mmHg
- Hypertensive Crisis: >180/>120 mmHg

Target Blood Pressure for Diabetes:
- General target: <140/90 mmHg
- Optimal target: <130/80 mmHg
- Individual targets may vary based on age and other conditions

Management Strategies:
- Lifestyle Modifications:
  * Reduce sodium intake (<2,300 mg/day)
  * DASH diet recommended
  * Regular physical activity
  * Maintain healthy weight
  * Limit alcohol consumption
  * Quit smoking

Monitoring Recommendations:
- Check BP at every routine diabetes visit
- Home monitoring if prescribed
- Record readings at different times of day
- Note any symptoms or triggers

When to Seek Medical Attention:
- Readings consistently above 140/90 mmHg
- Symptoms like severe headache, vision problems
- Readings above 180/120 mmHg (emergency)
- Unusually low readings with symptoms

Impact on Diabetes:
- High BP increases risk of diabetes complications
- May affect blood glucose management
- Regular monitoring of both BP and glucose important
- Medications may need adjustment based on both conditions
